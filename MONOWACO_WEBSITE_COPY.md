## CONTENT STYLE GUIDE

**Tone Characteristics:**

‚úÖ **Casual & Friendly**: Use contractions, conversational language, "you" and "your"
‚úÖ **Bold**: Make strong claims backed by specific benefits ("15 minutes vs. 4-6 hours")
‚úÖ **Educational**: Explain how things work, why they matter
‚ùå **Avoid**: Corporate jargon, overly technical language, vague promises, hype without substance

**Word Choice:**

- **Use**: "See exactly how clients engage" (specific)
- **Avoid**: "Advanced analytics capabilities" (vague)

- **Use**: "Create proposals in 15 minutes" (concrete)
- **Avoid**: "Streamline your workflow" (generic)

- **Use**: "Watch session recordings of client interactions" (clear benefit)
- **Avoid**: "Leverage behavioral intelligence" (jargon)

**Sentence Structure:**

- Keep sentences under 20 words when possible
- Use short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Break up text with subheadings, bullet points, and visual hierarchy
- Lead with benefits, follow with features

**Call-to-Action Language:**

- **Primary CTA**: "Start Your Free Trial" (action-oriented, clear)
- **Secondary CTA**: "See How It Works" / "Watch Demo" (low-commitment)
- **Avoid**: "Learn More" (vague), "Submit" (transactional)

**Proof Points:**

- Use specific numbers: "15 minutes" not "minutes"
- Use comparisons: "4-6 hours manually vs. 15 minutes with MonoWaco"
- Use client outcomes when available: "30% increase in close rate"
